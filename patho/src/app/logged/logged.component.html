What is PathoExtract ?

PathoExtract offers intuitive bioinformatics applications for the pre-processing and de novo assembly of Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) short read data. We are excited to announce our inaugural major application, PathoExtract: a fast and automated bioinformatics tool for preprocessing NGS data generated by Illumina sequencers. The provided functionalities include quality control, digital filtering (in silico depletion of host and other contaminating microorganisms), and assembly of the resulting reads

Tools

PathoExtract Receives Paired-End fastq data as input, whether compressed or not. Data quality is assessed using Fastqc and multiQC tools. Their qualities are adjusted if necessary. This step involves applying a set of treatments to correct observed artifacts. It results in the removal of a subset of reads or bases that do not meet a given criterion. This group of filters helps eliminate low-quality bases at the beginning and end of reads; remove reads with unknown bases (Ns), or reads with a length or average quality below a threshold.

During filtering, all reads from the quality control step are aligned to a human reference genome to subtract human contaminant reads. The non-aligned reads (decontaminated from human) then undergo a second alignment to the reference of the pathogen of interest, retaining only reads that correspond to it.

The reads that pass through the digital filtering are finally de novo assembled into larger sequences called contigs
